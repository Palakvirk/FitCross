Strength Building Program

Build muscle, increase strength, and improve overall fitness with our comprehensive strength training program. We offer exercises tailored to every fitness level from beginner to advanced.

Select Your Fitness Level:

Beginner Strength Program

Level: Beginner
Frequency: 3-4 days per week
Duration: 45-60 minutes
Recovery: 24-48 hours

This beginner program focuses on proper form and building a foundation of strength. Start with lighter weights and focus on mastering the movements.

Push-up

Chest Shoulders Arms
2-3 Sets
8-12 Reps
60s Rest

Instructions:

  1. Start in a plank position with hands shoulder-width apart.
  2. Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
  3. Lower your body until your chest nearly touches the floor.
  4. Push back up to the starting position.
  5. Repeat for the recommended number of repetitions.

Tips:

  • For an easier variation, perform push-ups with knees on the ground.
  • Keep your core engaged throughout the movement.
  • Don't let your hips sag or pike up.

Bodyweight Squat

Legs
2-3 Sets
12-15 Reps
60s Rest

Instructions:

  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart.
  2. Keep your chest up and core engaged.
  3. Bend your knees and push your hips back as if sitting in a chair.
  4. Lower until thighs are parallel to the floor (or as low as comfortable).
  5. Push through your heels to return to standing position.

Tips:

  • Keep your weight in your heels.
  • Ensure your knees track in line with your toes.
  • Maintain a neutral spine throughout the movement.

Dumbbell Row

Back
2-3 Sets
10-12 Reps
60s Rest

Instructions:

  1. Place one knee and hand on a bench with back flat and parallel to floor.
  2. Hold a dumbbell in your free hand with arm extended.
  3. Pull the dumbbell up to your rib cage, keeping your elbow close to your body.
  4. Slowly lower the dumbbell back to the starting position.
  5. Complete all reps on one side before switching.

Tips:

  • Avoid rotating your torso during the movement.
  • Focus on squeezing your shoulder blade at the top of the movement.
  • Use a weight that allows you to maintain proper form.

Intermediate Strength Program

Level: Intermediate
Frequency: 4-5 days per week
Duration: 60-75 minutes
Recovery: 48 hours

This intermediate program builds upon the fundamentals and introduces more compound movements and increased volume. Focus on progressive overload by gradually increasing weight or repetitions.

Barbell Bench Press

Chest
3-4 Sets
8-10 Reps
90s Rest

Instructions:

  1. Lie on a flat bench with feet firmly on the ground.
  2. Grip the barbell slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  3. Lower the bar to the middle of your chest while keeping elbows at a 45-degree angle.
  4. Press the bar back up to arms' length above the chest.
  5. Repeat for the recommended repetitions.

Tips:

  • Keep your wrists straight and elbows tucked at about 45 degrees.
  • Maintain contact with the bench with your head, upper back, and glutes.
  • Use a spotter when lifting heavier weights.

Barbell Back Squat

Legs
3-4 Sets
6-10 Reps
120s Rest

Instructions:

  1. Place a barbell across your upper back, resting on your trapezius muscles.
  2. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly turned out.
  3. Bend at the knees and hips to lower into a squat position.
  4. Lower until thighs are parallel to the floor or slightly below.
  5. Push through your heels to return to standing position.

Tips:

  • Keep your chest up and back straight throughout the movement.
  • Drive through your mid-foot and heels, not your toes.
  • Don't let your knees cave inward during the ascent.

Bent-Over Barbell Row

Back
3-4 Sets
8-10 Reps
90s Rest

Instructions:

  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, holding a barbell with hands just outside your legs.
  2. Hinge at the hips until your torso is almost parallel to the floor.
  3. Keep your back straight and core engaged.
  4. Pull the barbell up to your lower ribcage, keeping elbows close to body.
  5. Lower the barbell with control and repeat.

Tips:

  • Maintain a neutral spine position throughout the exercise.
  • Focus on pulling with your back muscles, not your arms.
  • Avoid using momentum to lift the weight.

Advanced Strength Program

Level: Advanced
Frequency: 5-6 days per week
Duration: 75-90 minutes
Recovery: 48-72 hours

This advanced program incorporates complex compound movements, periodization, and higher intensity techniques. Focus on proper form while pushing your limits for continued growth.

Barbell Deadlift

Back Legs
4-5 Sets
3-6 Reps
180s Rest

Instructions:

  1. Stand with feet hip-width apart, barbell over mid-foot.
  2. Bend at the hips and knees, grasp the bar with hands just outside your legs.
  3. Keep your back straight, chest up, and core engaged.
  4. Lift the bar by extending your hips and knees, keeping the bar close to your body.
  5. Stand up straight with shoulders back at the top of the movement.
  6. Return the weight to the floor by hinging at the hips and bending the knees.

Tips:

  • Drive through your heels and keep the bar close to your body throughout the lift.
  • Maintain a neutral spine position—don't round your back.
  • Engage your lats to help keep the bar close to your body.

Power Clean

Full Body
3-5 Sets
3-5 Reps
120s Rest

Instructions:

  1. Start with the barbell on the floor, feet hip-width apart.
  2. Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width.
  3. Begin with a deadlift motion, then explosively extend hips, knees, and ankles.
  4. Shrug your shoulders and pull the bar up as you drop under it.
  5. Catch the bar at the front of your shoulders in a quarter squat position.
  6. Stand up completely to finish the lift.

Tips:

  • Focus on explosive power from the hips rather than pulling with the arms.
  • Keep the bar close to your body throughout the movement.
  • Start with light weight to master the technique before progressing.

Weighted Pull-Up

Back
3-4 Sets
6-8 Reps
120s Rest

Instructions:

  1. Attach a weight belt with additional weight around your waist.
  2. Grip a pull-up bar with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  3. Hang with arms fully extended and feet off the ground.
  4. Pull your body up until your chin clears the bar.
  5. Lower your body with control until arms are fully extended.
  6. Repeat for the recommended repetitions.

Tips:

  • Maintain proper form despite the added weight.
  • Engage your core to prevent swinging.
  • Start with smaller weight increments and gradually increase.
