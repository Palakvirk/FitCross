Understanding Calorie Deficit

What is a Calorie Deficit?

A calorie deficit occurs when you consume fewer calories than your body burns in a day. This forces your body to use stored fat as energy, resulting in weight loss. One pound of fat contains approximately 3,500 calories, so a deficit of 500 calories per day should result in about 1 pound of weight loss per week.

Creating a Healthy Deficit

A moderate calorie deficit of 500-750 calories per day is generally considered safe and sustainable. Extreme deficits can lead to muscle loss, nutrient deficiencies, and metabolic slowdown. Focus on reducing calorie-dense, nutritionally-poor foods while maintaining adequate protein intake and consuming nutrient-rich whole foods.

Exercise and Deficit

While you can create a calorie deficit through diet alone, combining dietary changes with exercise offers additional health benefits. Exercise preserves muscle mass during weight loss, improves cardiovascular health, enhances mood, and increases your daily calorie expenditure. A combination of resistance training and cardio is ideal for most people.
